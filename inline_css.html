Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet to support life, with a dynamic environment shaped by water, atmosphere, and tectonic activity.

Physical Characteristics

Earth is an oblate spheroid with a circumference of about 40,000 kilometers (24,900 miles) and a diameter of approximately 12,700 kilometers (7,900 miles) at the equator, making it the largest and most massive of the rocky inner planets. Its mass is about 5.97×10²⁴ kilograms, and it is the densest planet in the Solar System. Earth’s surface is composed of 70.8% water and 29.2% land, including continents, islands, deserts, forests, and ice sheets. The planet’s crust is divided into tectonic plates, whose movements create mountains, volcanoes, and earthquakes.
