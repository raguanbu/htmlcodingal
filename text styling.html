Environmental pollution

Environmental pollution is the contamination of air, water, and land by harmful substances or energy, causing adverse effects on ecosystems, human health, and natural processes.

Definition and Overview

Environmental pollution occurs when harmful materials, known as pollutants, are introduced into the environment, disrupting natural processes and negatively affecting living organisms and ecosystems. Pollutants can be natural, such as volcanic ash, or human-made, like industrial waste, vehicle emissions, and plastic debris. Pollution can also involve energy forms, including noise, heat, light, and radioactivity, which can harm both humans and wildlife.

Types of Pollution

Air Pollution: Emission of gases and particulates, including carbon monoxide, sulfur dioxide, nitrogen oxides, and volatile organic compounds, which contribute to respiratory diseases and climate change.

Water Pollution: Contamination of rivers, lakes, oceans, and groundwater by chemicals, sewage, and plastics, leading to waterborne diseases and ecosystem disruption.

Land Pollution: Degradation of soil due to waste disposal, pesticides, fertilizers, and industrial chemicals, affecting agriculture and biodiversity.

Noise Pollution: Excessive sound from traffic, industry, and urban activities, causing stress, hearing loss, and wildlife disturbance.

Light Pollution: Mismanaged artificial lighting that disrupts ecosystems and human circadian rhythms.
